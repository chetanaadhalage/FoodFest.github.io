Maharashtra Famous Recipe : Aluvadi

Introducing your tea's best partner Alu vadi, also known as "pathrode" mostly famous in Maharashtra and Gujarat. Come try this ' loved by all but neglected by none' snack from us.



Ingredients:


  1. Eight leaves of Aluvadya (Alu of vegetables and Wadya is different, you should try it and ask.

  2. 1 cup gram dal flour

  3. 1 tablespoon cornflower

  4. Salt to taste

  5. Cumin 2 tbsp

  6. 1 teaspoon of white sesame seeds

  7. Tamarind pulp 1/4 cup

  8. 2 tbsp grated jaggery

  9. Oil for frying

  10. Wet coconut 1/2 cup

  11. 2 tablespoons red chili powder

  12. Pinch of asafoetida

  13. Wet coconut

  14. Cilantro

How To Make Aluvadi :



1.Add cornflour, red chili powder, salt, sesame seeds, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, asafoetida, tamarind pulp, jaggery and mix well.

2.Add a little water and soak till thick. Do not dilute the salt.

3.Aloo leaves should be washed and wiped clean. Stalk cutting. Spread a thin layer of the mixture on the back of the leaf by hand.

4.Invert the second page and spread the mixture.

5.Spread the mixture on 4 leaves one by one and fold the leaves inside on both sides and roll nicely.

6.While rolling, mix well.

7.Put oil in a cooker pot and cook the roll on steam for 10-15 minutes without blowing in the cooker.

8.Whistle 3 to 4 whistles.

9.It can be cooked outside without being put in the cooker.

10.When cool, cut into vertical slices and fry in hot oil or shallow fry.

11.Spread wet coconut and cilantro.
